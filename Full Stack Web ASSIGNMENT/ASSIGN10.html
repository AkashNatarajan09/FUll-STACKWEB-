Understanding HTML Entities and Comments

HTML Entities

HTML entities are used to display special characters that have reserved meanings in HTML. For example:
- The ampersand symbol: & (represented by &amp;)
- The less than symbol: < (represented by &lt;)
- The greater than symbol: > (represented by &gt;)
- The copyright symbol: © (represented by &copy;)
- The registered trademark symbol: ® (represented by &reg;)
- The trademark symbol: ™ (represented by &trade;)

Example Usage

Here is an example sentence using some of these special characters:

© 2024 MyWebsite®, All Rights Reserved. MyWebsite™ is a trademark of Example Corporation. For details, please visit our Terms & Conditions.
